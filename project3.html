Zoey and the Aliens

Agnesverse Log: “Zoey and the Aliens”

In this universe, baby Zoey is born on an Earth nearing destruction—overrun by buildings, technology, and devoid of space for humans. As aliens invade, they notice Zoey, the only baby who doesn’t cry, and take her with them. They raise her in their alien world, creating a robot friend to ease her loneliness. But Zoey feels isolated, wondering why no one looks like her and longing for connection.

Agnes’ Multiverse Manifesto: “Zoey and the Aliens”

Zoey’s story is about identity and belonging in a world that’s lost its humanity. Raised by aliens after Earth’s collapse, she struggles to find herself in a society that, despite its advancements, can’t understand her need for connection. This story reflects the growing sense of alienation in a rapidly changing world, where technology advances but genuine human connection fades. Through Zoey, I explore the deep desire to be seen, understood, and to belong—even in the most alien of places.
